Part II — Chapter 8

Playing Accurately, Part II: Hand vs. Distribution

How to make optimal decisions when you know your hand but not your opponent's

From Exposed Cards to Hidden Information

In real poker, we don't see our opponent's cards. Instead, we play our known hand against their distribution of possible hands. This chapter bridges the gap between the face-up analysis of Chapter 7 and real poker decision-making.

EV Against a Distribution

To calculate the EV of an action against an opponent's range, we evaluate against every hand they could hold, weighted by probability:

Distribution EV Formula <A, B> = Σ p(handi) × EV(our hand vs. handi)

We sum over all hands in the opponent's distribution, multiplying each hand's probability by our EV against that specific hand.

Worked Example: River Decision

Value Betting the River

You hold top pair on the river. Your opponent's range is:

Hand CategoryProbabilityResult if you bet and they call
Sets (beats you)10%Lose 1 bet
Two pair (beats you)15%Lose 1 bet
Top pair (ties/beats)25%Push or small loss
Middle pair (you win)15%Win 1 bet
Missed draws (folds)35%Win pot (no call)

Whether betting is +EV depends on how often worse hands call vs. better hands call. If only middle pair calls (15%) and sets/two pair always call (25%), you're getting called by better hands more often than worse — a bad value bet.

Thin Value Betting

Thin Value Bet

A bet that expects to show profit even when called, but only narrowly — you beat some of the opponent's calling range but lose to other parts. The key question: is EV(bet) > EV(check)?

Thin value betting is one of the most important skills that separates winning players from break-even ones. It requires accurate hand reading (distribution estimation) from Chapter 5.

The Importance of Combinatorics

Not all hand types are equally likely:

Hand TypeCombinationsExample
Pocket pair6AA = A♠A♥, A♠A♦, A♠A♣, A♥A♦, A♥A♣, A♦A♣
Suited hand4AKs = A♠K♠, A♥K♥, A♦K♦, A♣K♣
Offsuit hand12AKo = A♠K♥, A♠K♦, ... (4×3=12 combos)

There are twice as many AKo combos as AA combos. This dramatically affects distribution analysis.

Key Concepts

  • In real poker, we play our known hand against the opponent's distribution of possible hands.
  • EV is calculated by summing over all hands in the distribution, weighted by probability.
  • Thin value betting — betting when you beat some but not all of the calling range — is a key skill.
  • Combinatorics matter: there are 6 combos of a pair, 4 suited, and 12 offsuit combos of unpaired hands.
  • Against unknown opponents, use a default distribution and refine as you gather information.
