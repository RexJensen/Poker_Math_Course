Solving the Difference Equations

Mathematical derivation of the threshold formulas from Chapter 16

The Difference Equation

In Chapter 16, the analysis of no-fold [0,1] games produced a second-order homogeneous difference equation that describes the relationship between consecutive betting thresholds:

Equation 16.1 yk+2 + 2yk+1 − yk = 0

This equation governs where each player’s betting thresholds fall in the [0,1] game as the number of betting rounds increases. Each yk represents the hand strength at which a player transitions from one action to another.

General Solution Method

All equations of the form:

yn+2 + c·yn+1 + d·yn = 0

where c and d are constants, have a solution of the form:

Equation 16.1a yn = a·pn + b·qn

where a and b are constants determined by boundary conditions, and p and q are the real distinct roots of the characteristic equation:

x² + cx + d = 0

Finding the Roots

For our difference equation (c = 2, d = −1), the characteristic equation is:

x² + 2x − 1 = 0

Applying the quadratic formula:

x = −1 ± √2

This gives us two roots:

  • p = √2 − 1 ≈ 0.4142 — the “Golden Mean of Poker” from Chapter 14
  • q = −1 − √2 ≈ −2.4142
The Golden Mean of Poker (r)

We define r = √2 − 1. This constant appears throughout the book’s game-theoretic analysis. It is the positive root of x² + 2x − 1 = 0 and governs optimal bet sizing in many poker toy games.

Simplifying the Solution

Substituting the roots into the general solution:

yn = a·rn + b·(−1 − √2)n

A critical observation: as n → ∞, the thresholds yn must converge to 0 (the player needs an increasingly strong hand to justify additional bets). The second term diverges for any b ≠ 0, since |−1 − √2| > 1.

Boundary Condition Argument

Since yn → 0 as n → ∞, we must have b = 0. The solution simplifies dramatically to:

yn = a·rn

Finding the Constant a

Using the boundary condition y2 = 1 − y1:

ar² = 1 − ar

We use the identity r² = 1 − 2r (derived from r = √2 − 1):

a(1 − 2r) + ar = 1
a(1 − r) = 1
a = 1/(1 − r) = 1/(2 − √2)

Rationalizing: a = (2 + √2)/2 ≈ 1.7071

Final Threshold Formula

Threshold Formula

The betting thresholds in the no-fold [0,1] game are:

yn = (2 + √2)⁄2 · (√2 − 1)n

Each threshold is the previous threshold multiplied by r = √2 − 1.

Key Concepts

  • The difference equation yk+2 + 2yk+1 − yk = 0 governs no-fold [0,1] game thresholds
  • The characteristic equation method converts difference equations into closed-form solutions
  • The boundary condition yn → 0 eliminates the divergent term, simplifying the solution
  • Thresholds decrease geometrically by factor r = √2 − 1 per additional betting round
  • The constant r = √2 − 1 (“Golden Mean of Poker”) is fundamental to optimal play
  • The identity r² = 1 − 2r simplifies many calculations involving r
